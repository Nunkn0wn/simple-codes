Itachi Uchiha is considered a good character in the Naruto show for several reasons. One of the main reasons is his complex and tragic backstory, which is revealed gradually throughout the series. Itachi's past, including his childhood and his role in the Uchiha clan massacre, makes him a multi-dimensional character that viewers can sympathize with. Itachi's loyalty to his village and his dedication to protecting it at all costs, even if it means making personal sacrifices, is admirable. His actions, including his involvement in the Uchiha clan massacre, are controversial, but they are rooted in a desire to prevent a civil war that would have devastated the village. Finally, Itachi's interactions with other characters in the show, particularly his brother Sasuke, add depth to his character and create emotional moments that resonate with viewers. All these factors make Itachi Uchiha a well-written and compelling character in the Naruto show.
